Partnership model

Every collaboration begins with immersion.

We study your house codes, silhouettes, and future direction before the first stitch is mapped. Directors curate tactile essays—lacework studies, colour harmonies, metallic palettes—so the brief is understood through touch as well as sight.

Once approved, the commission enters an orchestrated cadence of prototypes, fittings, and rehearsals. Logistics are engineered in parallel to ensure ensembles travel safely and surface at the precise moment of presentation.

Process

How we work together

  1. Immersion. Share moodboards, archive references, and timelines. We host an in-person or digital salon to align on narrative, silhouette, and deliverables.
  2. Design development. Artwork, embroidery strike-offs, and material boards are produced in-house. Parcels of swatches and digital dossiers arrive for review and notation.
  3. Production & fittings. Handwork, machine embroidery, tailoring, and finishing proceed in parallel. Fittings can be staged in Mumbai, Paris, London, Milan, or New York.
  4. Assurance. Quality specialists examine every piece under runway lighting, logging notes in digital dossiers before sign-off.
  5. Arrival. Logistics teams coordinate customs, hand-carry couriers, and timed deliveries so your couture moment lands exactly when scheduled.
Infrastructure

All departments under one roof

Farruch Tower spans ten bespoke floors covering embroidery, design, accessories, QC, merchandising, export logistics, and the maison’s archive. Housing every discipline together keeps communication swift, confidential, and impeccably choreographed.

  • Dedicated embroidery floors for hand and machine disciplines.
  • Tailoring studios with moulage facilities for couture draping.
  • Accessory development labs for shoes, bags, and jewellery panels.
  • Climate-controlled archive with 20,000+ swatches for reference.
  • Export and dispatch centre linked to premium logistics partners.
